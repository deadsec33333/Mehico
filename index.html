Drug-Sniffer Dog Dies After Opioid Exposure at Mexican Border

Drug-sniffer dog Mya died after inhaling a potent opioid during a border search that uncovered automatic weapons, drugs, and $400,000 in cash.

Author of the article:
Kim Bolan
Published Nov 19, 2024 •  Last updated 10 minutes ago •  4 minute read

Article content

In a tragic incident at the U.S.-Mexico border, a drug-sniffer dog named Mya lost her life after inhaling a potent opioid during a routine vehicle inspection. The five-year-old Belgian Malinois was conducting a search on a suspicious van near a checkpoint in Arizona when she suddenly collapsed. Despite immediate medical intervention, Mya succumbed to the effects of the substance.

Customs and Border Protection (CBP) officers reported that the van had been flagged for secondary inspection due to irregularities in the driver’s behavior. Mya, a decorated member of the K-9 unit, was deployed to inspect the vehicle. During the search, she was exposed to airborne traces of a powerful opioid, suspected to be fentanyl.

Article content

Mya was rushed to an on-site veterinary team equipped with Narcan, an opioid-reversal medication for K-9 officers. Unfortunately, the dosage was not sufficient to counteract the exposure. CBP officials described the loss as "devastating," highlighting the dangers their canine teams face in the line of duty.

The driver of the van, a 38-year-old male, was immediately taken into custody. Authorities have yet to release his identity but confirmed he will face multiple federal charges. Mya's death has reignited discussions about the risks K-9 units encounter and the need for enhanced safety measures.

Mya was widely admired for her dedication, with over 150 successful narcotics detections during her career. "She was more than a partner; she was family," said one CBP officer, reflecting the sentiments of the entire team.

The inspection of the van following Mya’s tragic collapse uncovered a shocking array of illegal contraband. CBP agents discovered multiple automatic weapons, including AR-15-style rifles and ammunition, concealed in the vehicle's hidden compartments.

In addition to the weapons, agents seized over 20 pounds of illicit drugs, including heroin, cocaine, and fentanyl, packaged for distribution. Authorities estimate the street value of the drugs to be in the millions of dollars.

Also found in the van was $400,000 in cash, believed to be proceeds from drug trafficking operations. The driver now faces charges for weapons trafficking, drug possession with intent to distribute, and other serious offenses. Investigations are ongoing as authorities work to trace the origins of the contraband.

Mya’s sacrifice highlights the ongoing battle against drug and weapons smuggling at the border, a mission that continues despite the heavy toll it can take on those who serve.
